Early-century death rate per 100,000 (1900–1960) — before the measles vaccine

Approximate, from NCHS/Historical Statistics mortality-decline series via the partial Death Registration Area. Shows that case fatality fell sharply before vaccines (antibiotics, nutrition, supportive care). Both measles and pertussis death rates dropped ~95%+ over this window; the measles vaccine (1963) came after it.
